### Intro

激光剝離（Laser Lift-Off，LLO），該技術通過激光脈衝，將能量精確沉積到薄膜與基板的界面處。吸收的能量引發局部的熱或光化學反應，從而實現薄膜與基板的分離。可以利用該技術將生長於剛性基板上的 OLED 進行分離，並移植到柔性基板上實現可彎折的特性。

### Relate research
早期的激光剝離（LLO）工藝主要使用準分子激光作為脈衝光源，但其高成本和低穩定性限制了應用範圍。

2019 年，Steffen Bornemann 等人首次了使用飛秒級固態激光（520 nm）對 GaN 基 LED 進行 LLO 的可行性。實驗表明，剝離後的 GaN LED 結構完整，表面光滑，且保持穩定的電致發光性能，展示了固態激光作為替代光源的潛力。

2024 年，Stefan Wolter 等人進一步比較了高於能隙（347 nm）與低於能隙（520 nm）激光在 LLO 工藝中的表現。實驗顯示，520 nm 激光因能量穿透更深，而 347 nm 激光對結構損傷較小。此外，實驗表明脈衝寬度對熱影響起關鍵作用，子皮秒級脈衝能有效降低熱損傷，未引起顯著漏電流。

綜上，固態激光憑藉高穩定性和低損傷特性，成為更有優勢的激光來源


### Introduction

Laser Lift-Off (LLO) is a technique that uses laser pulses to precisely deposit energy at the interface between a thin film and its substrate. The absorbed energy induces localized thermal or photochemical reactions, resulting in the separation of the thin film from the substrate. This technology can be applied to detach OLEDs grown on rigid substrates and transfer them to flexible substrates, enabling bendable and flexible characteristics.

### Related Research

Early LLO processes primarily employed excimer lasers, such as KrF (248 nm) and ArF (193 nm), as the pulse source. However, their high cost and low stability limited broader applications.

In 2019, Steffen Bornemann et al. demonstrated the feasibility of using femtosecond solid-state lasers (520 nm) for LLO on GaN-based LEDs. Their experiments revealed that the GaN LED structures remained intact and smooth after detachment, maintaining stable electroluminescence performance. This highlighted the potential of solid-state lasers as an alternative light source for LLO.

In 2024, Stefan Wolter et al. compared the performance of LLO using above-bandgap (347 nm) and below-bandgap (520 nm) laser wavelengths. Their findings showed that 520 nm lasers caused more significant structural damage due to deeper energy penetration, introducing threading dislocation defects and increasing leakage current. In contrast, 347 nm lasers caused less structural damage. Furthermore, the experiments highlighted the critical role of pulse width in thermal effects.

In conclusion, solid-state lasers, with their high stability and low damage characteristics, have gradually replaced excimer lasers as the primary light source for LLO processes. This shift has provided crucial technical support for the efficient production of GaN-based LEDs.
